# Code of Conduct

This project explicitly aligns with a "values matter" stance rather than a "code 
is code, nothing else matters" approach. Contributions and continued 
participation are not evaluated on technical merit alone.

We regard the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, as well as the founding 
idea and goals of the European Union, as significant achievements worth 
defending. We reject and will not associate this project with the active 
support, promotion, or dissemination of narratives that justify human rights 
violations, that seek to undermine the human rights or dignity of any group of 
people, or that seek to legitimize violence and war.

We value freedom of belief and expression, including for views we may personally 
disagree with. Personal opinions and identity — regardless of background, 
origin, beliefs, or personal life choices — are never, in themselves, grounds 
for exclusion or discriminatory treatment within this project. The line we draw 
is not about what someone believes, but about whether their public conduct 
actively works to deny others their fundamental rights or freedoms, or to 
rationalize aggression against them.

If a contributor is found to actively and persistently promote such narratives 
— not as an isolated remark, but as a recognizable pattern of advocacy — we 
reserve the right to decline their contributions.

This is a boundary about what we are willing to associate this project with, not 
a permanent verdict on any individual. Positions and circumstances can change 
over time, and we see no value in exclusion that offers no way back — it tends 
to entrench division rather than resolve it.

Beyond that, we value a friendly and respectful tone in all project spaces. 
Insults, harassment, and discriminatory remarks toward others are not welcome 
here, regardless of who they are directed at. Disagreement and critical feedback 
are always fine. Just try to be a kind and decent person.
